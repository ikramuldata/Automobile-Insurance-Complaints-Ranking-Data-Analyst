This project analyzes automobile insurance complaint data published by the New York State Department of Financial Services (DFS). The objective is to evaluate insurance company performance, identify complaint trends, analyze premium growth patterns, and recommend operational improvements using data analytics and business intelligence techniques.

The analysis was performed using Microsoft Excel, MySQL, and Power BI to generate actionable insights from complaint and premium data between 2009 and 2016.

🎯 Objectives
Analyze complaint types and frequencies.
Identify complaint trends over time.
Evaluate insurance company performance using complaint ratios and rankings.
Analyze premium growth patterns.
Investigate factors influencing premium fluctuations.
Forecast future premium trends.
Build an interactive Power BI dashboard for insurer comparison.

📊 Dataset Information

The dataset contains automobile insurance complaint records and premium information for insurance companies operating in New York State.

Key Variables
Variable	Description
Complaint Ratio	Upheld complaints relative to premiums written
Upheld Complaints	Complaints ruled in favor of consumers
Question of Fact Complaints	Cases requiring further investigation
Not Upheld Complaints	Complaints where insurers were not found at fault
Premiums Written	Total premiums written by insurers
Rank	DFS complaint performance ranking

💡 Key Findings
Complaint Trends
Total complaint volumes declined significantly between 2009 and 2016.
Upheld complaints decreased by approximately 71%.
Complaint Distribution
Not Upheld Complaints accounted for 56% of all complaints.
Upheld Complaints represented only 12% of total complaints.
Premium Growth
Premiums increased from $9.71 billion in 2009 to $12.28 billion in 2016.
Overall premium growth was approximately 26.4%.
Company Improvement Analysis
Long Island Insurance Company was identified as requiring operational improvement due to:
High Complaint Ratio
High Upheld Complaint Volume
Poor DFS Ranking
Dashboard Insights
GEICO demonstrated strong premium performance with relatively low complaint ratios.
Allstate recorded the highest upheld complaint volume among selected companies.
